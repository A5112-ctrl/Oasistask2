Introduction


Lata Mangeshkar is an Indian playback singer and music director who was born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, India. She is considered to be one of the most prolific and popular singers in the Indian film industry, with a career spanning over seven decades. Lata Mangeshkar started her career in music as a child actor in Marathi films, and made her debut as a playback singer in the Hindi film "Aap Ki Seva Mein" in 1947. She rose to fame in the 1950s and 1960s with her melodious voice and ability to convey a wide range of emotions through her singing.

Lata Mangeshkar has been recognized for her philanthropic work as well, including her contributions to the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, a nationwide cleanliness campaign in India. She continues to be a prominent figure in Indian music, and her songs are still widely listened to and appreciated by fans around the world.

Timeline

  • 1929-Lata Mangeshkar is born on September 28 in Indore, India.
  • 1942-Lata Mangeshkar begins performing as a singer and actor in Marathi films.
  • 1947-Lata Mangeshkar makes her debut as a playback singer in the Hindi film "Aap Ki Seva Mein".
  • 1950- Lata Mangeshkar rises to fame as one of the leading playback singers in the Indian film industry, singing for numerous hit films.
  • 1958- Lata Mangeshkar records "Aayega Aanewala", a song from the film "Mahal", which becomes one of her most iconic and popular songs.
  • 1974-Lata Mangeshkar is awarded the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian honors.
  • 1989-Lata Mangeshkar is awarded the Dadasaheb Phalke Award, the highest honor in Indian cinema.
  • 2001- Lata Mangeshkar is awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • 2012-Lata Mangeshkar is featured on the cover of the Guinness World Records book for having recorded the most songs in the world.
  • 2020-Lata Mangeshkar releases a new version of the song "Ek Pyar Ka Nagma Hai" as a tribute to her late brother Hridaynath Mangeshkar on his birthday.

    • Today, Lata Mangeshkar is considered to be one of the greatest playback singers in the history of Indian cinema, and her contributions to Indian music have been recognized and celebrated around the world.
